2 Corinthians: Chapter 1-13



Paul writes a letter of reconciliation to the church at Corinth, and clears up some concerns that they have. Author: Paul. During a trying time in his interactions with the Corinthian church, Paul penned his Second Letter to the Corinthians. Evidently, some churchgoers had harshly criticized Paul and his job, yet he reveals both his profound yearning for reconciliation and his immense satisfaction when it is achieved. Paul explains why he had reacted harshly when he was resisted and insulted by certain Corinthian church members in the first section of the letter, and he expresses his satisfaction that this harshness had brought about repentance and reconciliation. Then he makes a hefty giving request to the church in order to aid the struggling Christians in Judea. In the closing chapters, Paul confronts a small group of Corinthian individuals who had presented themselves as genuine apostles and accused Paul of being a fraudulent one.

Outline of Contents
Introduction 1:1-11
Paul and the church at Corinth 1:12-7:16
The offering for the Christians in Judea 8:1-9:15
Paul's defence of his authority as an apostle 10:1-13:10
Conclusion 13:11-14
