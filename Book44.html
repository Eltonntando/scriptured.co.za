Acts: Chapter 1-28



Jesus returns to the Father, the Holy Spirit comes to the church, and the gospel of Jesus spreads throughout the world. Author: Luke. Luke is continued in The Acts of the Apostles, which is also devoted to the enigmatic Theophis. Its main goal is to describe how Jesus' early supporters disseminated the Good News about him "in Jerusalem, and in all of Judea and Samaria, and to the ends of the earth" under the guidance of the Holy Spirit (13). It tells the tale of the early Church, the Christian movement that got its start among Jews and eventually spread throughout the world. In addition, the author wanted to convince his audience that Christians were not a political threat to the Roman Empire and that Christianity was the fulfillment of Jewish tradition. Acts is divided into three main sections, which represent the constantly expanding territory in which the Good News about Jesus was spread and the Church established: (1) The start of the Christian movement in Jerusalem after Jesus' ascension (1:1–8:3); (2) The expansion into Samaria, Syria, and other nearby communities (8:4–12:25); and (3) The further expansion into the Mediterranean region as far as Rome, with a focus on Paul's missionary work (13:1–28:31). The activity of the Holy Spirit, who descends with power upon the Jerusalem believers on the day of Pentecost and continues to lead and bolster the Church and its leaders throughout the events detailed in the book, is a significant aspect of Acts. The early Christian message is Summarized in a number of sermons, and the events recorded in Acts show the power of this message in the lives of the believers and in the fellowship of the Church.
